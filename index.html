About LedgerbackØDCRC

The Ledgerback Digital Commons Research Cooperative (LedgerbackØDCRC) is a nonprofit cooperative and p2p network for fostering collaboration between stakeholders and unifying the study of the internet and society (i.e., the network society) to advance towards a global technological commonwealth.

Mission

Our mission is to:

  1. mobilize citizens, teachers, students, tutors, course developers, and researchers together to positively transform and interconnect online learning and discussions about metascience (funding, collaboration, open science, publishing, etc.),
  2. address issues and problems we have identified in educational technology and scholarly discussion (especailly around metascience) to rebalance the relationships between stakeholders and between technology and stakeholders, and
  3. positively transform both areas by combining social constructivism, platform cooperativism, mechanism design and Web 3.0 technologies

Cooperative Model

As stated above, we plan to implement Laplace as a multi-stakeholder cooperative that gives a voice to citizens, students, teachers, researchers, technologists, tutors, and course developers. We believe a cooperative is the apporpriate model to engage these stakeholders because the interaction of these stakehodlers will create a collective good (knowledge commons) while taking into account the individual interests and preferences of each stakeholder. Additionally, we do not believe that any area in the knowledge commons (from ideation to deliverable) is truly separate and thus, each stakeholder's opinion is necessary to reach resolutions that benefit the collective overall.

Lastly, a cooperative ensures that the members can determine how accrued value is redistributed and that the people who work on the platform have a say over how they work.

Social Model

Our social model is based on social constructivism and on how new scientiffic breakthroughs occur. In both circumstances, it is the interaciton betwene individuals concerning knwoedlge and building off pre-existing knowledge through association that leads to the creation and expansion of knowledge. Thus, we have chosen to make Laplace a social learning and scholarly hub to encourage interaction among users and thus lead to an expansion and deeper understanding of knowledge. In this model, we utilize both traditional categories and user-created tags to group and find knowledge and allow users to customize existing knowledge products to better meet their own needs and help others expand their horizons.
